# Financial Data Extraction Methodology & Architecture

This document serves as the technical backbone and methodology outline for the automated extraction of financial metrics from approximately 3,000 corporate Annual Reports (PDFs) across over 300 diverse corporate entities. 

The dataset generated by this pipeline is intended to support academic and quantitative research. As such, this document outlines the end-to-end architecture, strict heuristic rules, data provenance guarantees, and post-processing firewalls that ensure the integrity and precision of the extracted data.

---

## 1. Introduction & Executive Summary

The primary objective of this project is to accurately extract 37 highly complex financial metrics from unstructured and semi-structured Financial Statements and Annual Reports (PDFs). The target corpus spans a wide range of industry archetypes, including:
* **Banks & Financial Institutions**
* **Real Estate Investment Trusts (REITs) and Developers**
* **Pure-Play IT & Software Services**
* **Heavy Manufacturing and Logistics**
* **Tech Startups and Quick Commerce**

### The Solution: Multi-Stage, Dual-Scope LLM Architecture
To achieve high precision comparable to a senior financial analyst, we deploy a multi-stage architecture powered by the Google Gemini Vision API, leveraging File Context Caching. The system parses full PDFs, identifies relevant textual and tabular intersections, and evaluates candidates against strict accounting rules.

---

## 2. The Extraction Architecture

The architecture operates in three distinct phases:

### Stage 1: Multi-Candidate Generation (Recall)
Instead of attempting to immediately pinpoint a single "correct" answer, the extraction engine utilizes the Gemini Context Cache to scrape *all* potential matching candidates for a specific metric. 
* The engine captures the candidate value, the verbatim source text surrounding it, the physical page number, and the section type (e.g., *Management Discussion & Analysis (MD&A)*, *Board's Report*, *Statutory Financial Statements*).

### Stage 2: Layer 2 Verification (Precision)
This is the "Agentic Analyst" layer. The system evaluates all generated candidates against a rigorous set of accounting heuristics. For example:
* **Statutory Precedence:** Formal audited statements (P&L, Balance Sheet, Cash Flow) and primary Notes hold absolute precedence over voluntary infographic highlights or MD&A summaries. 
* **Scope Conviction:** Candidates are evaluated to ensure their page headers (e.g., "Consolidated Statement of Profit and Loss") match the intended extraction scope.

### Stage 3: Deterministic Post-Processing Firewalls
Before writing the final data, the pipeline enforces strict code-level safeguards (Python heuristics) to prevent math and logic violations:
* **EBIT vs. EBITDA Guard:** Ensures that $EBIT$ is strictly less than or equal to $EBITDA$.
* **Duplicate Margin Exclusion:** If a single line item is erroneously captured for both EBIT Margin and EBITDA Margin (e.g., resulting in identical values), the guard rejects the duplication. A normalizer strips varying formats (e.g., `8.3` vs `8.3%`) to ensure identical values are caught reliably.
* **Adjusted EBITDA Validation:** Identical values for EBITDA and Adjusted EBITDA are rejected unless the source text explicitly contains adjustment keywords (e.g., "exceptional", "one-time").

---

## 3. Dual-Scope Processing: Consolidated vs. Standalone

Corporate disclosures require strict segregation between parent company operations (**Standalone**) and the entire group including subsidiaries (**Consolidated**). The engine processes these independently:

* **Scope Leak Prevention:** Structural headers (e.g., tables embedded in Directors' Reports) often report consolidated ratios even when the surrounding text discusses standalone operations. The extraction engine evaluates running page headers to prevent standalone scope contamination.
* **CARO Exclusions:** Under the Companies (Auditor's Report) Order (CARO), specific metrics (like Cash Loss status) apply strictly and exclusively to standalone parent reports. The engine automatically rejects any Consolidated Cash Loss candidates sourced from a CARO report.
* **Trust Parity Rules:** For REITs, Consolidated and Standalone Net Distributable Cash Flow (NDCF) values can differ by microscopic amounts (e.g., `0.01M`) due to rounding in SPV-level inputs. The extractor ensures Standalone values are not carelessly copied to Consolidated sheets.

---

## 4. Data Provenance & Audit Trails

To ensure the resulting dataset is research-grade, traceability is built into the core pipeline:

* **JSON Audit Logs (`_POC3.json`):** Every processed PDF yields an Excel workbook alongside a detailed JSON audit log. 
* **Granular Traceability:** Researchers can trace every single extracted data point back to its exact coordinate in the source PDF. The dataset provides the `Physical Page Number` and the `Verbatim Source Text` for every metric.
* **Phase 3 Visual Verification:** The audit trail allows human auditors (or parallel LLM agents) to conduct rapid, forensic spot-checks directly on the physical pages to verify numerical accuracy and scope conviction without reading the entire document.

---

## 5. Domain-Specific Adaptability

Financial metrics vary significantly by industry. The system's prompt definitions natively adapt to different domains out-of-the-box:

* **Real Estate & IT:** Capable of extracting "Bookings" or "Pre-sales" values specific to developer volume or Total Contract Value (TCV) in software.
* **Cash Flow vs. Debt:** Extracts "Net Surplus Cash" automatically when companies hold negative Net Debt.
* **Banking Treasury:** Filters out non-relevant operating metrics in favor of banking-specific treasury and net interest income logic.

---

## 6. Performance & Quality Assurance

In benchmark testing (Batch 3 of the POC), the system processed 68 complex PDFs spanning a diverse range of companies and years.

* **Heuristic Precision:** Out of 311 non-null target metrics extracted across both Consolidated and Standalone sheets, the system achieved a verified accuracy of **98.71%**.
* **Continuous Integration:** Errors identified during visual audits (such as formatting bypasses in the margin guards) were translated into deterministic code-level tests (e.g., `_normalize_val` in Python) to prevent regressions on future batches.

---

## 7. Limitations and Known Edge Cases

Researchers using this dataset should be aware of the following known constraints:

1. **PDF Quality & Corruption:** Documents missing standard `EOF` markers or suffering from severe corruption may fail parsing.
2. **Heavy Scans vs. Native Text:** While Gemini Vision handles image-based PDFs well, heavily distorted scans or extremely dense nested tables spanning multiple pages can occasionally reduce candidate generation recall.
3. **LLM Spatial Reasoning:** Extracting values from complex tables where row headers are physically distant from data columns can pose challenges, though Layer 2 verification mitigates the risk of accepting the wrong candidate.
4. **Accounting Standard Variations:** The pipeline is highly optimized for Indian accounting standards (Ind AS / IGAAP) and specific auditor reporting formats (e.g., CARO). Applying the pipeline to non-Indian jurisdictions (e.g., US GAAP or IFRS) may require prompt tuning for jurisdictional nuances.
